Blog Post One
10/16/2023
I learned a whole lot about HTML and CSS in this assignment! This little project is the first real experience I have with HTML and CSS. The tools of web development are a lot less intimidating to me than they felt to me before starting this course. I like the markup language and I think so far it has been pretty intuitive to me. I wouldn’t say that I have encountered any significant challenges in making this webpage, just about any place I got stuck I was able to resolve pretty quickly by playing around with values and doing a bit of googling. Though to be fair, I am not doing anything especially complicated. I am most proud of being able to separate my main page into two halves, I think it looks much nicer than my selfie being below the text. By far the most useful resource in constructing this webpage would be the lectures I have so far attended on web development. I’ve also found a lot of very helpful information on the Ed Discussion forums.
Blog Post Two
10/30/2023
I am quite proud of what I have been able to accomplish with this assignment. The screenshots of what we needed to transform the webpage into seems daunting at first, but as I chipped away at the various requirements I could quickly see it coming together. This assignment was much more challenging than the first, but I do not believe that the pace of the class is getting away from me. I think, in fact, that the pace is just about right to really dive into web development. We have explored a lot more of web development a lot sooner than I expected we would. The most challenging part of this second assignment was attempting to right-justify the text on each of the posts. They are still center aligned, so I was never able to solve this issue. I think it has something to do with the fact that the three elements of text under each post are concatenated to the previous by a 'span' tag. This causes the elements to behave in ways that I do not understand.
